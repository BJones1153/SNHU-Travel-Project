# SNHU-Travel-Project

How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?

Interpreting user needs starts with listening carefully to stakeholders and understanding what they are trying to accomplish rather than focusing only on technical requirements. In the SNHU Travel project, user feedback about personalization and preferred destinations helped translate general ideas into specific features such as the Top Five Destination List and personalized recommendations. Creating user stories made this process much clearer because they forced me to frame requirements from the user’s perspective. Instead of thinking about code first, I had to think about what the user wanted to achieve. Acceptance criteria then helped define when a feature was complete, which made implementation more structured and measurable.

How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

I approach program development by breaking large problems into smaller, manageable parts. The Scrum-Agile framework reinforced this mindset by organizing work into user stories and incremental tasks. Rather than attempting to complete an entire system at once, I focused on one feature at a time and tested it before moving forward. In future development work, I hope to continue using backlog prioritization, iterative development, and regular reflection. The concept of continuous improvement and adaptability is especially valuable because software projects often evolve over time.

What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

Being a good team member in software development means communicating clearly, taking responsibility for assigned tasks, and supporting collaboration. During this project, I practiced communicating clearly when asking for clarification on user stories and acceptance criteria. Even though this was an academic simulation, it demonstrated the importance of maintaining transparency and keeping others informed of progress or obstacles. A strong team member also respects different roles within the Scrum framework and understands that each role contributes to delivering value to the end user.
